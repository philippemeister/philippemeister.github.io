Courselink

Research and design by Philippe Meister

Problem

How do you translate and education into a career? Some students are very successful at this translation. Some are in professional degrees that lend themselves to this translation. But some, like humanities students, may struggle to make this translation and apply their edcution in professional spaces.

Research

I interviewed over 20 recruiters, students, academic advisors, and career services. The interviews helped me see the cracks in how students engage with career services at the university. Through transcribing and anayzing the interviews, I learned that students aren’t always prepared to make good use of career services. A reoccuring pattern is that students come in to career services in their third year when they look for internships or their fourth year when they look for jobs. Students and career centers need to spend time reflecting on the previous three years and identifying the students relevant strengths.

Design

We can help students pepare to use career services by helping them recognize and demonstrate their knowledge, skills, and abilities as they go through college. Users can import courses from from their university’s learning management system. After the courses are imported, users can describe the knowledge, skills, and abilities they associate with the experience. Next, they can upload artifacts of their work. Additionally, users can add other types of experiences, such as work experiences. They system will aggregate and visualize the experince for users. After the user has input their epxerinces they can use their experince to find new experinces. They can browse job listings and compare their current knoweldge, skills, and abilites to the job listing.
