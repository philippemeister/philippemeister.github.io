Philippe Meister's Portfolio


My portfolio illustrates my ability to reserach, design, and evaluate human-computer interactions in the context of product development. I am experienced in conducting user-centered design processes, which puts users at the center of product design and evaluation. I am skilled at conducting user reserach, iterative design, and user evaluations. The portfolio items showcase my work to research, design, and evaluate users' experiences in aviation and insurance domains.


Thunderstorm Visualization

This work designed and evaluated a 3D AR thunderstorm visaulization for general aviation weather training (part 1 of 3).

Thunderstorm Learning Activities

This work designed and evaluated augmented reality (AR) learning activities for GA weather training (part 2 of 3).

Thunderstorm Learning Module

This work designed an AR-enhanced learning module and evaluated it against a traditional module (part 3 of 3).

Account User Interfaces

This work designed and evaluated new user interfaces for accounts and policies in the IMT Platform.

Journey Mapping

This work mapped agents' experiences submitting commerical business to IMT Insurance.

GVL Design System

This work developed a the foundations of a design system for GVL's web-based products.

Snow Stomper

This work designed and evaluated a snow removal tool that shifts the stress of shovening from the back to the legs, which could decrease back injuries.

Courselink

This work designed a platform that helps college students students track their professional development and use what they have learned to plan their career.

Kitchen Builder

This work designed a kitchen building tool that was used to test whether users prefer low- or high-fidelity graphics for their initial room-generation workflow.
