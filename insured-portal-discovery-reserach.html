Insured Portal Discovery Research

The work is summarized below. More detail is available upon request.

Introduction

The objective of this project was to generate internal knowledge of the current usage of the insured portal (MyPolicy+) and inform design decisions. An insured portal is an online system used in the insurance industry to provide insureds with access to information and services. Insureds’ usage of their insured portal can impact their level of satisfaction with their carrier (JD Power, 2022). Therefore, IMT wanted to know how we could provide insureds with higher-quality user experiences in the insured portal.

Approach

A five-step research project was conducted to gather information about the insured portal:

  • Step 1: Related work: Find and review internal documentation about our insured portal and industry literature about insured portals
  • Step 2: Interface comparison: Compare various insured portals
  • Step 3: Agent research: Conduct interviews with agents to discover whether they have any issues with or want additional functionality in the portal
  • Step 4: Insured research: Survey current MyPolicy+ users to generate knowledge about their uses of the system, levels of satisfaction, and needs for additional functionality
  • Step 5: Draft recommendations: Draft a list of recommendations for the insured portal

Results and recommendations

The research found that IMT can increase the number of insureds using the portal and improve their experience of using it. The recommendations were the following:

  • Better promote the portal to insureds and agents to increase use
  • Optimize the portal for online billing and allow insureds to pay multiple accounts at one time
  • Provide insureds with the ability to order road services and file an auto claim
  • Provide insureds with their agent’s contact information
  • Consider providing insureds the ability to renew, request changes, and edit drivers and cars
  • Update the visual styling to the IMT design system styles
  • Increase the use of plain language in the information architecture
  • Provide additional functionality to support new business processes
